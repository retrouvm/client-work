Achimota Forest Conservation

14 Years of Tireless Advocacy for Ghana's Last Remaining Forest in Accra

For approximately 14 years, Dr. Brenya Twumasi has been at the forefront of efforts to protect Achimota Forest, the only remaining forest area with precious ecology in Accra. Beginning with walks through the forest in 1971, Dr. Twumasi has maintained a deep connection to this vital ecosystem and its community.

Team members and advocates have spent many sleepless nights writing to each President, land commissioner, court, and media for over 12 years. Despite decades of advocacy work, the forest continues to face threats from government development and illegal encroachment through corruption. This work represents a critical fight for Ghana's national development and environmental preservation.

As Dr. Twumasi emphasizes: "It's not about Achimotaâ€”it is rather the last forest that was present in Accra is close to extinction. We as humans were tasked to Tend the Gardens. We begun Advocacy in Earnest 12 years ago - some of us have a long-standing Agricultural background. We saw this as vital. Many were shortsighted despite our efforts."

Sustainable vegetation, trees, and gardens have been a focal point since establishing C4C (Perfect Peace C) in 1973. The people must stand up and support those who tirelessly work on this matter. We are so divided, but unity is essential for protecting our natural heritage.

Botanical Gardens & Educational Programs

Organized Tours and Educational Programs by Dr. Brenya Twumasi - May 2025

Dr. Brenya Twumasi has organized comprehensive tours and educational programs at major botanical gardens in Ghana, including:

  • Achimota School Forest - The last remaining forest in Accra
  • Achimota School Farms - Educational agricultural programs
  • Achimota School Botanical Garden - Plant conservation and education
  • University of Ghana Botanical Gardens - Research and conservation
  • National: Aburi Botanical Gardens - Historic botanical preservation

These programs provide students and community members with essential knowledge about sustainable agriculture, forest conservation, and environmental stewardship. Many students at Achimota School had not previously heard about the school's farms and botanical garden, highlighting the importance of these educational initiatives.

C4C: Perfect Peace C

Sustainable Vegetation, Trees, and Gardens - A Focal Point Since 1973

Since establishing C4C (Perfect Peace C) in 1973, sustainable vegetation, trees, and gardens have been a central focus of Dr. Brenya Twumasi's work. This long-standing commitment to environmental stewardship and agricultural sustainability forms the foundation of her consulting practice.

The organization's work encompasses forest conservation, sustainable agriculture, botanical garden management, and environmental advocacy. Through decades of dedicated effort, C4C has been instrumental in raising awareness about the importance of preserving Ghana's natural heritage and promoting sustainable agricultural practices.
