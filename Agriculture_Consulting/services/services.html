Botanical Garden Tours & Educational Programs

Organized by Dr. Brenya Twumasi - May 2025

Dr. Brenya Twumasi organizes comprehensive tours and educational programs at major botanical gardens throughout Ghana. These programs provide essential knowledge about sustainable agriculture, forest conservation, and environmental stewardship.

Locations include:

  • Achimota School Forest - The last remaining forest in Accra, featuring precious ecology and biodiversity
  • Achimota School Farms - Educational agricultural programs demonstrating sustainable farming practices
  • Achimota School Botanical Garden - Plant conservation and educational programs
  • University of Ghana Botanical Gardens - Research-focused botanical conservation and education
  • National: Aburi Botanical Gardens - Historic botanical preservation and public education

These educational initiatives help students and community members understand the importance of sustainable agriculture, forest conservation, and environmental stewardship. Many students discover these valuable resources for the first time through these organized programs.

Achimota Forest Conservation Services

14 Years of Advocacy and Conservation Work

Dr. Brenya Twumasi provides specialized consulting services for forest conservation, with particular expertise in the protection of Achimota Forest. This work includes:

  • Advocacy Strategy Development - Comprehensive strategies for protecting forest ecosystems through government engagement, legal action, and public awareness
  • Community Engagement - Building coalitions and engaging communities in forest conservation efforts
  • Documentation & Media - Professional documentation of forest conditions, threats, and conservation efforts through film, photography, and written reports
  • Policy Development - Guidance on developing and implementing policies for forest protection
  • Educational Programs - Raising awareness about the importance of forest conservation and environmental protection

This work represents a critical component of Ghana's national development, addressing environmental issues, illegal encroachment, and the urgent need for forest protection. The fight to protect Achimota Forest continues, and support from communities and stakeholders is essential.
