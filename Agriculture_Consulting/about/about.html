Dr. Brenya Twumasi

Agricultural Consultant & Environmental Advocate

Dr. Brenya Twumasi brings decades of experience in sustainable agriculture, environmental conservation, and agricultural development. Her work spans forest conservation, botanical garden management, fisheries and aquaculture development, and sustainable farming practices.

Since 1973, Dr. Twumasi has been dedicated to sustainable vegetation, trees, and gardens through her work with C4C (Perfect Peace C). This long-standing commitment to environmental stewardship forms the foundation of her consulting practice and advocacy work.

Beginning in 1971, Dr. Twumasi developed a deep connection with Achimota Forest, walking through it regularly and building relationships with the community. This connection has fueled over 14 years of tireless advocacy to protect Achimota Forest, the only remaining forest area with precious ecology in Accra.

Achimota Forest Advocacy

Protecting Accra's Last Remaining Forest

For approximately 14 years, Dr. Brenya Twumasi has been at the forefront of efforts to protect Achimota Forest. This work has involved countless hours of advocacy, including writing to Presidents, land commissioners, courts, and media outlets. The fight continues as the forest faces ongoing threats from development and illegal encroachment.

As Dr. Twumasi emphasizes: "It's not about Achimotaâ€”it is rather the last forest that was present in Accra is close to extinction. We as humans were tasked to Tend the Gardens. We begun Advocacy in Earnest 12 years ago - some of us have a long-standing Agricultural background. We saw this as vital. Many were shortsighted despite our efforts."

The documentary work and advocacy surrounding Achimota Forest represents a critical component of Ghana's national development conversation, addressing environmental issues, illegal encroachment, politics, and the urgent need for environmental protection.

C4C: Perfect Peace C

Sustainable Vegetation, Trees, and Gardens Since 1973

C4C (Perfect Peace C) was established in 1973 with a focus on sustainable vegetation, trees, and gardens. This organization has been instrumental in promoting environmental stewardship, agricultural sustainability, and conservation efforts throughout Ghana.

The work of C4C encompasses forest conservation, sustainable agriculture practices, botanical garden management, and environmental education. Through decades of dedicated effort, C4C has raised awareness about the importance of preserving Ghana's natural heritage and promoting sustainable agricultural practices.
